Welcome to Our website!

We are Game of King's and our mission is to help you to be the best you can be! For the last 20 years our founder's and creater have been playing in tournament's all across the globe.After many win's and just as many losses, we decided that the one thing stopping us from practicing and playing the game we love even more, was how obscure and difficult it can be to find an easy, accessible way to enter tournaments, especially at a beginner level.

Our Goal and our endgame is to help where we can to make this fantastic game even better and even more accessible to all ages, you're never too old to learn to play and it one of the few games that never gets old. Feel free to check out below where we explain some basic rules behind chess and help everyone from beginners starting out to advanced players needing to brush up on skills.

King x 1

Kings move one square in any direction, so long as that square is not attacked by an enemy piece. Additionally, kings are able to make a special move, know as castling.

Queen x 1

Queens move diagonally, horizontally, or vertically any number of squares. They are unable to jump over pieces.

Knight x 2

Knights move in an ‘L’ shape’: two squares in a horizontal or vertical direction, then move one square horizontally or vertically. They are the only piece able to jump over other pieces.

Bishop x 2

Bishops move diagonally any number of squares. They are unable to jump over pieces.

Rook x 2

Rooks move horizontally or vertically any number of squares. They are unable to jump over pieces. Rooks move when the king castles.

Pawn x 8

Pawns move vertically forward one square, with the option to move two squares if they have not yet moved. Pawns are the only piece to capture different to how they move. Pawns capture one square diagonally.
